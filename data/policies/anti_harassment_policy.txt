ANTI-HARASSMENT AND NON-DISCRIMINATION POLICY
==============================================

Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Last Updated: January 15, 2025
Policy Number: HR-POL-006

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes the company's zero-tolerance stance on harassment, discrimination, and retaliation. It applies to all employees, contractors, interns, vendors, and visitors.

2. SCOPE
This policy covers conduct in all work-related settings, including:
- Company premises and facilities
- Work-related travel and events
- Electronic communications (email, chat, social media)
- Remote work environments
- Client and vendor interactions

3. PROTECTED CATEGORIES
The company prohibits harassment and discrimination based on:
- Race, color, ethnicity, national origin
- Sex, gender, gender identity, gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Age (40 and over)
- Religion, creed, beliefs
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Veteran or military status
- Marital or familial status
- Pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions
- Genetic information
- Any other characteristic protected by applicable law

4. DEFINITIONS

4.1 Harassment:
Unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that:
- Creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment
- Unreasonably interferes with work performance
- Affects employment decisions (quid pro quo)

Examples include:
- Verbal: Slurs, epithets, jokes, derogatory comments, threats
- Physical: Unwanted touching, blocking movement, physical intimidation
- Visual: Offensive images, gestures, objects, written materials
- Electronic: Harassing emails, texts, social media posts

4.2 Sexual Harassment:
Any unwelcome sexual advance, request for sexual favors, or other verbal/physical conduct of a sexual nature, including:
- Quid pro quo: Employment decisions conditioned on sexual conduct
- Hostile environment: Severe or pervasive conduct that creates an intimidating work environment

4.3 Discrimination:
Treating employees differently based on protected characteristics in areas of:
- Hiring and termination
- Compensation and benefits
- Promotions and assignments
- Training and development
- Performance evaluations

5. REPORTING PROCEDURES

5.1 How to Report:
Employees experiencing or witnessing harassment/discrimination should report to:
- Direct supervisor or manager
- Human Resources
- Anonymous ethics hotline: 1-800-555-ETHICS
- Online reporting portal: ethics.company.com
- Title IX Coordinator (for sexual harassment)

5.2 Investigation Process:
- All complaints are taken seriously and investigated promptly
- Investigation begins within 3 business days of report
- Confidentiality maintained to the extent possible
- Typical investigation timeline: 30-60 days
- Written findings and conclusions provided to parties

5.3 No Retaliation:
- Retaliation against anyone reporting harassment or participating in an investigation is strictly prohibited
- Retaliation constitutes a separate policy violation subject to disciplinary action

6. DISCIPLINARY ACTION
Violations may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Mandatory training
- Suspension (with or without pay)
- Demotion or reassignment
- Termination of employment
- Legal action

7. PREVENTION MEASURES
- Mandatory annual anti-harassment training for all employees
- Additional training for managers and supervisors
- Regular policy communications and reminders
- Workplace culture surveys and assessments
- Bystander intervention training

8. ACCOMMODATION AND SUPPORT
- Employees affected by harassment may request reasonable accommodations
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP) available for counseling
- No negative impact on performance reviews related to reporting

---
Compliance: Title VII, ADA, ADEA, state and local anti-discrimination laws.
Contact: HR Department at hr@company.com or ethics hotline at 1-800-555-ETHICS.
