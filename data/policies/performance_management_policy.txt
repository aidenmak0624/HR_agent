PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT POLICY
==============================

Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Last Updated: January 15, 2025
Policy Number: HR-POL-010

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes a consistent, fair, and developmental approach to performance management across the organization.

2. PERFORMANCE CYCLE
- Q1 (January-March): Goal setting and development planning
- Q2-Q3 (April-September): Mid-year review and continuous feedback
- Q4 (October-December): Year-end review, calibration, and ratings
- Ongoing: Regular 1-on-1 meetings (minimum bi-weekly)

3. GOAL SETTING (SMART Framework)
All goals must be:
- Specific: Clearly defined outcome
- Measurable: Quantifiable metrics or milestones
- Achievable: Realistic given resources and constraints
- Relevant: Aligned with team and company objectives
- Time-bound: Defined deadline or timeline

Recommended Goal Mix:
- 60% Business/Performance goals
- 20% Development/Growth goals
- 20% Culture/Collaboration goals

4. 360-DEGREE FEEDBACK
- Self-assessment required for all employees
- Manager assessment required
- 2-3 peer reviewers recommended
- Direct report feedback for managers (anonymous)
- Skip-level feedback for senior leaders
- All feedback subject to bias screening before inclusion

5. RATING SCALE
- 5 (Exceptional): Consistently exceeds all expectations; top 5-10%
- 4 (Exceeds Expectations): Frequently exceeds expectations; top 15-25%
- 3 (Meets Expectations): Consistently meets all expectations; 50-60%
- 2 (Needs Improvement): Inconsistently meets expectations; 10-15%
- 1 (Unsatisfactory): Does not meet expectations; bottom 5%

6. CALIBRATION PROCESS
- Department-level calibration sessions conducted in Q4
- Facilitated by HR Business Partners
- Rating distribution reviewed for consistency
- Prevents rating inflation/compression
- Adjustments documented with rationale

7. PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLANS (PIP)
- Required for employees rated "Needs Improvement" or "Unsatisfactory"
- Duration: 30, 60, or 90 days based on severity
- Clear improvement areas, success criteria, and check-in schedule
- Manager and HR jointly develop and monitor PIP
- Outcomes: Successful completion OR further action (up to termination)

8. BIAS PREVENTION
- All written reviews scanned for biased language before submission
- Calibration sessions review for demographic patterns in ratings
- Annual pay equity analysis cross-referenced with performance ratings
- Manager training on unconscious bias in evaluations
- Bias detection tools flag concerning language in feedback

9. DOCUMENTATION
- All reviews, goals, and feedback archived in HR system
- Records retained for duration of employment + 3 years
- Employees receive copies of all finalized reviews
- Rating history informs talent management decisions

10. APPEALS PROCESS
- Employees may appeal final ratings within 14 days
- Appeals reviewed by skip-level manager and HR
- Decision communicated within 10 business days
- Appeal documentation added to employee file
