CODE OF CONDUCT AND ETHICS POLICY
===================================

Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Last Updated: January 15, 2025
Policy Number: HR-POL-008

1. PURPOSE
This code defines the ethical standards and professional behavior expected of all company employees, contractors, and representatives.

2. CORE VALUES
- Integrity: Act honestly and transparently in all business dealings
- Respect: Treat all individuals with dignity and fairness
- Accountability: Take responsibility for actions and decisions
- Excellence: Strive for quality and continuous improvement
- Collaboration: Work together constructively

3. PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT
- Maintain professional demeanor in all interactions
- Avoid conflicts of interest; disclose potential conflicts to management
- Protect confidential and proprietary information
- Use company resources responsibly and for legitimate purposes
- Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and company policies

4. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
- Outside employment must not conflict with company duties
- Financial interests in competitors, suppliers, or customers must be disclosed
- Family relationships affecting business decisions must be disclosed
- Gifts and entertainment must comply with company gift policy (max $100 value)

5. CONFIDENTIALITY
- Trade secrets, customer data, and business strategies are confidential
- Non-disclosure obligations survive employment termination
- Unauthorized disclosure may result in termination and legal action
- Social media posts must not reveal confidential information

6. USE OF COMPANY RESOURCES
- Company equipment, email, and systems are for business purposes
- Incidental personal use is permitted if it does not interfere with work
- Company reserves the right to monitor electronic communications
- No expectation of privacy on company systems

7. REPORTING VIOLATIONS
- Report suspected violations to manager, HR, or ethics hotline
- Whistleblower protections apply to good-faith reports
- Anonymous reporting available via 1-800-555-ETHICS

8. CONSEQUENCES
- Violations subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Serious violations may be reported to law enforcement
- Annual certification of compliance required from all employees
