WORKPLACE SAFETY AND HEALTH POLICY
====================================

Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Last Updated: January 15, 2025
Policy Number: HR-POL-007

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes the company's commitment to providing a safe and healthy workplace in compliance with OSHA and applicable state regulations.

2. EMPLOYER RESPONSIBILITIES
- Provide a workplace free from recognized hazards
- Comply with OSHA standards and regulations
- Provide necessary safety training and equipment
- Maintain records of workplace injuries and illnesses
- Post OSHA notices and safety information

3. EMPLOYEE RESPONSIBILITIES  
- Follow all safety rules and procedures
- Use required personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Report unsafe conditions and incidents immediately
- Participate in safety training programs
- Refrain from operating equipment without proper training

4. INCIDENT REPORTING
- All workplace injuries, illnesses, and near-misses must be reported within 24 hours
- Report to: immediate supervisor and Safety Officer
- Emergency situations: Call 911, then notify management
- OSHA-reportable incidents documented on Form 300

5. ERGONOMIC SAFETY
- Workstation assessments available for all office employees
- Ergonomic equipment provided upon request with medical documentation
- Remote workers eligible for home office ergonomic assessment
- Standing desk options available

6. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
- Evacuation plans posted at all exits
- Fire drills conducted quarterly
- First aid kits maintained in all work areas
- AED devices located on each floor
- Emergency contact list maintained and distributed

7. WORKERS' COMPENSATION
- All workplace injuries covered under workers' compensation insurance
- Employees must report injuries promptly to qualify
- Modified duty assignments available when medically appropriate
- Return-to-work program supports gradual reintegration

---
Compliance: OSHA Act of 1970, state workplace safety regulations.
Contact: Safety Officer at safety@company.com.
