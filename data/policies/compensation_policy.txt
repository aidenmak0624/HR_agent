COMPENSATION AND PAY EQUITY POLICY
=====================================

Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Last Updated: January 15, 2025
Policy Number: HR-POL-009

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes fair, equitable, and transparent compensation practices aligned with market data and internal equity principles.

2. COMPENSATION PHILOSOPHY
- Pay competitively at market median (50th percentile) for base salary
- Total compensation targets 60th-75th percentile with incentives
- Pay equity reviews conducted annually across all protected categories
- Transparent pay bands published for all job levels

3. PAY STRUCTURE
3.1 Job Levels and Pay Bands:
- Individual Contributor: IC1 through IC6
- Management: M1 through M4
- Executive: E1 through E3
- Each level has a defined pay range (minimum, midpoint, maximum)
- Employees typically start between minimum and midpoint

3.2 Base Salary:
- Based on job level, experience, skills, and market data
- Annual market data survey benchmarking
- Geographic pay differentials for remote workers

3.3 Variable Pay:
- Individual performance bonuses (0-20% of base salary)
- Team/department incentives
- Company-wide profit sharing
- Spot bonuses for exceptional contributions (up to $5,000)

4. PAY EQUITY
- Annual pay equity analysis across gender, race, age, and disability
- Statistical regression analysis to identify unexplained pay gaps
- Remediation budget allocated for equity adjustments
- Third-party audit conducted every two years
- Results reported to board compensation committee

5. MERIT INCREASES
- Annual merit increase budget: typically 3-5% of payroll
- Increases tied to performance rating:
  - Exceptional: 5-8%
  - Exceeds: 4-6%
  - Meets: 2-4%
  - Needs Improvement: 0%
  - Unsatisfactory: 0% (may be placed on PIP)

6. PROMOTIONS
- Promotion increases: typically 8-15% above current salary
- Must be within new level's pay band
- Requires manager recommendation and HR approval
- Effective on first of the month following approval

7. SALARY TRANSPARENCY
- Pay bands published on internal HR portal
- Employees may request their position within the pay band
- Pay philosophy communicated during onboarding
- Prohibited from retaliation for discussing pay (NLRA compliance)

---
Compliance: Equal Pay Act, Title VII, state pay transparency laws.
