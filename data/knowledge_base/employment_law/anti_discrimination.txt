ANTI-DISCRIMINATION AND ANTI-HARASSMENT POLICY
TechNova Inc. | Effective January 1, 2026
===========================================================

POLICY STATEMENT
TechNova Inc. is committed to providing a workplace free from discrimination and harassment. We prohibit discrimination and harassment based on any legally protected characteristic. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, clients, and visitors.

PROTECTED CHARACTERISTICS (FEDERAL LAW)
Under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act, the ADA, ADEA, Equal Pay Act, Pregnancy Discrimination Act, and other federal laws, discrimination is prohibited based on:
- Race and color
- Religion (including religious dress and grooming practices)
- Sex (including pregnancy, childbirth, breastfeeding, and related medical conditions)
- Sexual orientation and gender identity
- National origin
- Age (40 and older, under the ADEA)
- Disability (physical or mental, under the ADA)
- Genetic information (under GINA)
- Veteran status
- Citizenship status (under IRCA)

DEFINITION OF HARASSMENT
Harassment is unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that:
- Is made a condition of employment (quid pro quo), OR
- Creates a work environment that a reasonable person would consider intimidating, hostile, or abusive

Examples of prohibited conduct include:
- Slurs, epithets, or derogatory comments related to a protected class
- Offensive jokes, images, or gestures
- Physical threats, intimidation, or assault
- Unwanted sexual advances or requests for sexual favors
- Displaying offensive materials in the workplace or on company devices
- Cyberbullying or online harassment through company systems

SEXUAL HARASSMENT
Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when:
- Submission is made a term or condition of employment
- Submission or rejection is used as the basis for employment decisions
- The conduct unreasonably interferes with work performance or creates a hostile environment

REPORTING PROCEDURES
Employees who experience or witness discrimination or harassment should report it promptly through any of these channels:
1. Direct manager (if not the source of the complaint)
2. HR Department: hr@technova.com or ext. 2100
3. VP of People Operations: emily.rodriguez@technova.com
4. Anonymous ethics hotline: 1-800-555-0188 (available 24/7)
5. Online reporting form on the HR portal

INVESTIGATION PROCESS
1. Acknowledgment: HR will acknowledge receipt of the complaint within 2 business days
2. Investigation: HR will conduct a prompt, thorough, and impartial investigation
3. Standard: Preponderance of evidence (more likely than not)
4. Interviews: Complainant, accused, and relevant witnesses will be interviewed
5. Timeline: Investigations are typically completed within 20-30 business days
6. Findings: Both parties will be notified of the outcome
7. Remedial Action: If the complaint is substantiated, appropriate corrective action will be taken

CONFIDENTIALITY
All complaints and investigations are handled with discretion. Information is shared only on a need-to-know basis to conduct a thorough investigation.

ANTI-RETALIATION
TechNova strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Files a good-faith complaint of discrimination or harassment
- Participates as a witness in an investigation
- Opposes practices they reasonably believe to be discriminatory

Retaliation includes adverse employment actions such as termination, demotion, schedule changes, or exclusion from meetings or projects.

PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE FOR VIOLATIONS
Employees found to have engaged in discrimination, harassment, or retaliation will be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination. Depending on severity:
1. Verbal counseling and documented warning
2. Written warning placed in personnel file
3. Suspension without pay
4. Termination of employment

Severe violations (e.g., physical assault, quid pro quo sexual harassment) may result in immediate termination.

EXTERNAL REPORTING OPTIONS
Employees also have the right to file a complaint with:
- Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC): www.eeoc.gov or 1-800-669-4000
- State fair employment practices agency
- Filing deadline: Generally 180 days from the discriminatory act (300 days in states with a fair employment agency)

TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
- All employees complete anti-harassment training within 30 days of hire
- Annual refresher training required for all employees
- Managers receive additional training on recognizing, preventing, and responding to harassment
