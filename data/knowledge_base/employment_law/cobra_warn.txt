SECTION 1: COBRA (CONSOLIDATED OMNIBUS BUDGET RECONCILIATION ACT)

OVERVIEW
COBRA provides employees and their dependents who lose health benefits the right to choose to continue group health coverage for a limited period. TechNova, as an employer with 50+ employees, is subject to COBRA requirements.

QUALIFYING EVENTS FOR EMPLOYEES
The following events trigger COBRA eligibility:
- Voluntary or involuntary termination of employment (except for gross misconduct)
- Reduction in hours that causes loss of coverage

QUALIFYING EVENTS FOR DEPENDENTS
Spouses and dependent children may be eligible for COBRA upon:
- Death of the covered employee
- Divorce or legal separation from the covered employee
- Employee becoming entitled to Medicare
- Dependent child ceasing to be eligible under the plan (e.g., aging out)

COVERAGE DURATION
- Termination or reduction in hours: 18 months
- Disability (Social Security determination): Up to 29 months (18 + 11 month extension)
- Death, divorce, Medicare eligibility, or loss of dependent status: 36 months

COST
- COBRA participants pay up to 102% of the full group health plan premium (employer + employee share + 2% administrative fee)
- Current approximate monthly COBRA premiums at TechNova:
  - Employee only: $750/month
  - Employee + spouse: $1,450/month
  - Employee + family: $2,100/month
- Payment is due on the 1st of each month with a 30-day grace period

NOTICE AND ELECTION
- TechNova will provide a COBRA election notice within 14 days of receiving notification of a qualifying event
- Employees have 60 days from the qualifying event or the date of the COBRA notice (whichever is later) to elect COBRA coverage
- Coverage is retroactive to the date of the qualifying event if elected

HOW TO ENROLL
1. You will receive a COBRA Election Notice via mail within 14 days of your qualifying event
2. Complete and return the Election Form within 60 days
3. Submit your first premium payment within 45 days of election
4. Contact the Benefits Administrator: benefits@technova.com or ext. 2105

ALTERNATIVES TO COBRA
Before electing COBRA, consider:
- Health Insurance Marketplace (healthcare.gov): May offer lower premiums with subsidies
- Spouse's employer plan: Qualifying event allows special enrollment
- Medicaid: If income qualifies

SECTION 2: WARN ACT (WORKER ADJUSTMENT AND RETRAINING NOTIFICATION ACT)

OVERVIEW
The WARN Act requires employers with 100 or more full-time employees to provide 60 calendar days advance written notice before a plant closing or mass layoff.

WHEN WARN APPLIES
- Plant Closing: Shutdown of a single site of employment resulting in 50+ employee job losses during any 30-day period
- Mass Layoff: Job loss at a single site of 500+ employees OR 50-499 employees if they represent at least 33% of the workforce at that site

NOTICE REQUIREMENTS
TechNova will provide written notice to:
- Affected employees or their union representatives
- The state dislocated worker unit
- The chief elected official of the local government
Notice must be provided at least 60 calendar days before the event.

EXCEPTIONS TO 60-DAY NOTICE
- Faltering company: Employer actively seeking capital that would avoid layoff, and giving notice would jeopardize the effort
- Unforeseeable business circumstances: The closing or layoff is caused by business circumstances not reasonably foreseeable at the time notice would have been required
- Natural disaster: Earthquake, flood, drought, storm, tidal wave, or similar events

PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
- Back pay and benefits for each day of violation (up to 60 days) for each affected employee
- Civil penalty of up to $500 per day to the local government (if no notice provided)

STATE MINI-WARN LAWS
Some states have additional WARN requirements with lower thresholds:
- California: 75+ employees, any layoff of 50+ employees
- New York: 25+ employees, layoff of 25+ employees or 33% of workforce
- Illinois, New Jersey: Various additional requirements
TechNova complies with the most protective applicable law.

EMPLOYEE RIGHTS DURING LAYOFFS
If a layoff occurs, affected employees receive:
- 60 days advance written notice (or pay in lieu of notice)
- Continuation of health benefits through COBRA
- Outplacement assistance and career counseling
- Severance pay per TechNova's Severance Policy (see Employee Handbook)
- Access to EAP services for 90 days post-separation
