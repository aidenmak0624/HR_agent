401(K) RETIREMENT PLAN AND STOCK COMPENSATION
TechNova Inc. | Effective January 1, 2026
===========================================================

SECTION 1: 401(K) RETIREMENT PLAN

PLAN OVERVIEW
TechNova offers a 401(k) retirement savings plan to help employees build long-term financial security. The plan is administered by Fidelity Investments and offers a generous employer match.

ELIGIBILITY
- All full-time and part-time employees working 20+ hours per week
- Eligible to enroll immediately upon hire (no waiting period)
- Employer match begins after 90 days of employment

CONTRIBUTION LIMITS (2026)
- Employee contribution limit: $24,500 per year
- Catch-up contribution (age 50+): Additional $7,500 per year (total $32,000)
- Combined employee + employer limit: $70,000 per year

EMPLOYER MATCH
TechNova provides a dollar-for-dollar match on the first 4% of your eligible compensation, plus 50 cents on the dollar for the next 2%:
- Contribute 1%: TechNova matches 1% (total: 2%)
- Contribute 2%: TechNova matches 2% (total: 4%)
- Contribute 3%: TechNova matches 3% (total: 6%)
- Contribute 4%: TechNova matches 4% (total: 8%)
- Contribute 5%: TechNova matches 4% + 0.5% = 4.5% (total: 9.5%)
- Contribute 6%: TechNova matches 4% + 1% = 5% (total: 11%)
- Maximum employer match: 5% of eligible compensation

AUTO-ENROLLMENT
New employees are automatically enrolled at a 6% contribution rate (to maximize the employer match) unless they opt out or change their rate. The default investment is the age-appropriate Target Date Fund.

VESTING SCHEDULE
- Employee contributions: 100% vested immediately (always yours)
- Employer match: 3-year graded vesting
  - After 1 year: 33% vested
  - After 2 years: 67% vested
  - After 3 years: 100% vested

INVESTMENT OPTIONS
The plan offers a range of investment options through Fidelity:
- Target Date Funds (2030, 2035, 2040, 2045, 2050, 2055, 2060)
- S&P 500 Index Fund (expense ratio: 0.015%)
- Total Stock Market Index Fund (expense ratio: 0.02%)
- International Stock Index Fund (expense ratio: 0.06%)
- Bond Index Fund (expense ratio: 0.03%)
- Small Cap Index Fund (expense ratio: 0.05%)
- Company Stock Fund (limited to 10% of balance)
- Stable Value Fund (guaranteed rate: ~3.5%)

ROTH 401(K) OPTION
Employees may choose to make some or all contributions on a Roth (after-tax) basis. Same contribution limits apply. Qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

LOANS AND HARDSHIP WITHDRAWALS
- 401(k) Loans: Borrow up to 50% of vested balance (maximum $50,000). Repayment over 1-5 years via payroll deduction. Interest rate: Prime + 1%.
- Hardship Withdrawals: Available for immediate and heavy financial need (medical expenses, home purchase, tuition, funeral, eviction prevention). Subject to 10% early withdrawal penalty if under 59.5.

ROLLOVERS
- Incoming rollovers from previous employer plans or IRAs are accepted at any time
- Outgoing rollovers upon separation processed within 5-7 business days

SECTION 2: EQUITY COMPENSATION

RESTRICTED STOCK UNITS (RSUs) - For Eligible Employees
- Grant Eligibility: Senior engineers, managers, directors, and above
- Standard Vesting: 4-year schedule with 1-year cliff
  - Year 1: 25% vests on the 1-year anniversary
  - Years 2-4: Remaining 75% vests in equal quarterly installments (6.25% per quarter)
- Tax Treatment: RSUs are taxed as ordinary income upon vesting
- TechNova withholds taxes at the supplemental income rate at time of vesting

STOCK OPTIONS (Early-Stage Equity)
- Grant Type: Incentive Stock Options (ISO) where eligible; Non-Qualified Stock Options (NSO) otherwise
- Exercise Period: 10 years from grant date
- Post-Termination Exercise: 90 days after separation to exercise vested options
- ISO Tax Advantage: No ordinary income tax at exercise (may trigger AMT); capital gains treatment on sale if holding period met (1 year from exercise, 2 years from grant)

EMPLOYEE STOCK PURCHASE PLAN (ESPP)
- Eligibility: All employees working 20+ hours/week after 6 months
- Discount: Purchase TechNova stock at 15% discount from fair market value
- Contribution: Up to 15% of base salary (maximum $25,000/year)
- Purchase Periods: Semi-annual (January 1 and July 1)
- Lookback Provision: Price is the lower of the start or end of the purchase period, minus 15%
